Hurricane Ivan

Geographic Location

Hurricane Ivan started near Northern Africa and traveled across the Atlantic Ocean. It demolished through the Caribbean islands, especially Grenada, Jamaica, and the Cayman Islands, then went on to hit Cuba. After crossing the Gulf of Mexico, it crashed into the United States near Gulf Shores, Alabama, close to the Florida border.

Event Data & Description

Ivan reached the U.S. at 2:10 a.m. on September 16, 2004. It neared the shore as a Category 3 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson scale, which rates hurricanes from 1, being the weakest, to 5, being the strongest.

Ivan was a beast — at one point it reached Category 5 strength with winds thundering at 165 mph. It hit Alabama with winds at a high of 130 mph. The storm resulted in 124 deaths and caused a massive $21 billion in damage. In Grenada, a tiny island nation, Ivan managed to damage almost every building. It was the most expensive hurricane ever recorded for both Grenada and the Cayman Islands.

Fun facts

  • Extremely long strength: Ivan stayed crazy strong for 7 days straight — that’s a world record for keeping top wind speeds that high without a break.
  • It came back! After hitting Alabama, Ivan’s leftover energy looped back into the ocean, reformed into a much weaker tropical storm, and hit Louisiana a second time on September 24.
  • Tornado creator: Ivan created 111 tornadoes as it moved over land, ripping through parts of the southeastern U.S.
  • Grenada experienced severe damage to power lines, resulting in around 80% of power lost.
